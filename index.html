The Invicta 4-Step Framework
Every objection, every time — in this order:
1. Acknowledge — Validate what they said. Never argue. Never defend.
2. Clarify — Ask a question to understand more. Slows them down, gives you information.
3. Bridge — Reframe the objection. Give them a reason to keep listening.
4. Return to Script — Get back to your process. Ask for the DOB. Keep moving.
The goal is never to “win” the objection. It’s to keep the conversation alive long enough to help them.

Objection Handling Practice
Each scenario simulates a real inbound lead call. The client’s lines are taken directly from Invicta’s objection handling cheat sheet. Speak your response out loud — the app scores you on whether you hit the key phrases from the framework.
→ The client speaks first
→ Speak your response naturally after they finish
→ The app detects your keywords and scores each step
→ At the end you get a breakdown and AI coaching feedback
Tip: Don’t memorise word for word. Learn the logic of each step, then say it in your own words.

Active Tasks
Practice “Can’t afford it” ×3
Due this week · Target 70+
The most common objection on initial contact calls. Clarify whether it’s cashflow or value before you respond — the answer is completely different depending on which it is.
Cost objections feel like dead ends but they’re usually not. Advisers who can separate a cashflow problem from a value problem convert significantly more leads. This is one of the highest-leverage scenarios to get right.
Practice “Already sorted” ×2
Due this week · Target 70+
Clients with existing cover aren’t closed doors — they’re often paying more than they need to or missing cover. Find out what they have before deciding how to respond.
A lot of advisers hear “already sorted” and give up. The ones who ask a follow-up question almost always find a gap. This scenario trains you to make that move confidently without seeming pushy.
Full first meeting run-through
Due this week · Target 65+
Run the entire Digital First Script end-to-end with an AI client. This is how you build confidence in the full flow — not just individual objections.
Objection handling is only one part of the call. Advisers who practise the full meeting flow handle transitions more naturally and keep momentum through the whole fact find. Do this weekly.
Opening & Introduction ×3
Due this week · Target 70+
Drill the Opening section of the Full Meeting script until you can deliver it without thinking. The first 60 seconds sets the tone for the whole call.
A weak opening creates doubt early. A strong opening builds instant rapport and gets the client to lower their guard. This is worth practising until it sounds completely natural — not scripted.
Disclosure section ×2
Due this week · Target 65+
Practice the Disclosure section until you can deliver it smoothly without losing the client’s attention. Disclosure needs to feel like part of the conversation, not a legal read-out.
Advisers who rush through or stumble on Disclosure come across as nervous or untrustworthy. A confident, natural Disclosure actually builds credibility — it signals you’re organised and professional.
KiwiSaver switch conversation ×3
Due this week · Target 65+
Practice identifying the KiwiSaver opportunity, asking the right questions, and making the pivot to NZ Funds feel natural and value-driven rather than salesy.
KiwiSaver is one of the most impactful conversations you can have with a client. The difference in returns between a bank KiwiSaver and NZ Funds can be hundreds of thousands of dollars over 20–30 years. Learn to make that case confidently and specifically.
Handle “Can you call me back?” ×3
Due this week · Target 60+
Practice keeping leads on the line. Callbacks rarely convert. The goal is to buy yourself 5 more minutes right now, not to schedule a call that won’t happen.
Most advisers agree to callback and then never reach the client again. Practising this changes your default response from “sure, when works?” to “it’s only 5 minutes — is now actually bad?”. That shift alone will save you leads.
Medical history section ×2
Due this week · Target 65+
Drill the Fact Find Pt 2 section of the Full Meeting. Medical questions feel awkward if you’re not used to asking them. Practice until you can ask them directly and without hesitation.
Medical questions are where advisers often go quiet or apologise for asking. That signals discomfort to the client. Confident, direct questions in this section build trust and get better answers — which leads to better applications.

Your Training Path
XP is earned every time you complete a session. The more sessions you complete and the higher you score, the faster you level up.
🎯
Level 1 — Trainee (0–199 XP)
You’re learning the framework. Focus on one step at a time. Don’t try to nail all four steps in your first 10 sessions. Get the Acknowledge right first, then layer the rest in.
📚
Level 2 — Developing (200–499 XP)
You understand the structure. Now work on consistency. Aim to score 60+ across every scenario before moving on. Pick your two weakest scenarios and repeat them until they improve.
⚡
Level 3 — Practitioner (500–999 XP)
You’re consistent. Start pushing for 70+ across the board. Run full meeting sessions, not just individual scenarios. You should be able to handle any initial contact objection without hesitation.
⭐
Level 4 — Senior (1000–1999 XP)
Your scores are strong and consistent. Focus on KiwiSaver and referral scenarios — that’s where the biggest business impact is. Start helping newer advisers with their technique.
🥇
Level 5 — Elite (2000+ XP)
Top of the leaderboard. You’re a reference point for the team. Keep practising to maintain your edge — the advisers below you are closing the gap. Set the standard by showing up every day.
